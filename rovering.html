THE HISTORY OF ROVERING
IN THE WORLD

*The Rover programme had its orgin in 2 different ways.The first, aimed in the boy Scouts
in the United Kingdom who were aged between 15 and 18 years old, was called "senior scouts"
which was launched in march 1917 during world war.
*The second scheme was the series of 'Battlefield Scout Huts' provided for recreation of
British and empire soldiers in rear areas of the western fort. Related to these St George's
Scout Club for service men,which operated in the English garrison town of Colchester under the
leadership of "Uncle"H.Geoffrey Elwes.
*From these projects,it became apparent that ther was a need for a Soting related programme that
catered for young men.The first mention of the term "Rover scouts" was by Baden-Powell in the boy
Scout headquaters Gazatte in August 1918 and was fully established by November 1919. BP set about
ritting a handbook for the new scheme, wich was published in 1922 as "Rovering To Success".
